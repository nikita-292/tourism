About Taj ul Masjid

Taj ul Masjid, located in Bhopal, is one of the largest mosques in India. It stands as a symbol of grandeur and architectural brilliance. The construction of this mosque was initiated by Shah Jahan Begum, the ruler of Bhopal, but it was later completed by her daughter, Sultan Jahan Begum, in 1971.

The mosque is an architectural marvel, featuring a stunning facade adorned with intricate carvings and beautiful domes. The grandeur of Taj ul Masjid is truly a sight to behold, making it a must-visit for tourists and history enthusiasts alike.

Visiting Taj ul Masjid

Location: Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, India
Timings: Open daily from sunrise to sunset
Entry Fee: Free

Getting There

Taj ul Masjid is easily accessible by road from various parts of Bhopal. The nearest railway station is Bhopal Junction, and the nearest airport is Raja Bhoj Airport.
