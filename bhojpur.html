About Bhojpur Temple

Bhojpur Temple, located near Bhopal, is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is situated on the banks of the Betwa River and is known for its historical and architectural significance. It stands as a testament to the rich cultural and religious heritage of the region.

The temple is believed to have been commissioned by the legendary Parmara King, Raja Bhoja, in the 11th century. However, the construction of the temple remains incomplete, making it a unique and intriguing archaeological site.

History of Bhojpur Temple

Bhojpur Temple is named after Raja Bhoja, who was a patron of art, culture, and architecture. According to historical accounts, the construction of the temple was halted due to unknown reasons, possibly including a scarcity of funds or a change in the political scenario during that time.

The temple is particularly famous for the colossal Shiva Lingam installed inside. This Lingam, made of a single stone, is considered one of the largest in India. Despite the incomplete structure, the temple attracts devotees and tourists alike who come to witness its unique architectural features and pay homage to Lord Shiva.

Getting There

Bhojpur Temple is easily accessible by road from Bhopal. It is approximately 28 kilometers away from the city. Visitors can hire local transportation or drive to reach this sacred destination.
