About Moti Masjid

Moti Masjid, also known as the Pearl Mosque, is an exquisite mosque located in Bhopal. The mosque is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and stands as a testament to the cultural and historical heritage of the region.

Moti Masjid was commissioned by Sikander Begum, the daughter of Qudsia Begum, in the 19th century. The name "Moti Masjid" translates to the Pearl Mosque, and the mosque derives its name from the glistening white marble used in its construction.

History of Moti Masjid

Moti Masjid was built between 1868 and 1901 during the reign of Sikander Begum. The mosque was designed by the British architect Henry Irwin and is renowned for its architectural beauty and symmetry. The use of white marble and the meticulous detailing contribute to the mosque's timeless elegance.

Moti Masjid served as a place of worship for the royal family and the public. It has been a cherished landmark in Bhopal, attracting visitors with its serene ambiance and architectural grandeur.
