About Van Vihar National Park

Van Vihar National Park, situated in Bhopal, is a wildlife sanctuary that spans across a lush green area. The park is known for its diverse flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for nature lovers, wildlife enthusiasts, and tourists alike.

The park is home to a variety of indigenous and migratory species, providing a serene environment for visitors to connect with nature. Van Vihar aims to promote conservation, education, and recreation in a sustainable manner.

History of Van Vihar National Park

Van Vihar National Park was established in 1983 with the objective of conserving and protecting wildlife. The park covers a significant area on the banks of the Upper Lake in Bhopal and was developed as a combination of a national park and zoo.

Over the years, Van Vihar has become a hub for wildlife conservation efforts, rescue and rehabilitation of animals, and environmental education. The park provides a natural habitat for various species and serves as an important green lung in the heart of Bhopal.

Visiting Van Vihar National Park

Location: Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, India
Timings: Open from sunrise to sunset
Entry Fee: Varies for Indian and foreign visitors, check with the official website for details

Getting There

Van Vihar National Park is easily accessible by road from different parts of Bhopal. The park provides a peaceful retreat for those looking to immerse themselves in the beauty of nature and observe wildlife in a natural setting.
